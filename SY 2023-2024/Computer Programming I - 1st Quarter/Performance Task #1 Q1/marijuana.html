What Is Cannabis?

Cannabis, also known as marijuana among after names, is a psychoative drug from the cannabis plant. Native to Central or South Asia, the cannabis plant has been used as a drug for both recreational and enthogenic purposes and in various traditional medicines for centuries.

The Tetrahyrocannabinol (THC) is the main psychoactive component of cannabis, which is just one of the 483 known compounds in the plant. Cannabis can be used in widely different ways, such as: smoking, vaporizing within food, or as an extract.
